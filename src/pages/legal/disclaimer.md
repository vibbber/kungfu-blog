## When to Seek Professional Help

The Service is not equipped to handle serious or urgent situations. **Please contact a qualified professional immediately** if your child or family is experiencing any of the following:

- **Safety concerns:** Any situation involving physical danger, abuse, neglect, or self-harm
- **Mental health crises:** Suicidal thoughts or statements, severe anxiety or depression, psychotic symptoms, or sudden personality changes
- **Eating disorders:** Persistent food refusal, binge eating, purging, or extreme food restriction beyond normal picky eating
- **Developmental concerns:** Significant regression in speech, motor skills, or social development; suspected autism spectrum disorder; suspected learning disabilities
- **Behavioral escalation:** Persistent aggression, violence toward self or others, property destruction, or behavior that poses a safety risk
- **Medical symptoms:** Any physical health concerns, injuries, or symptoms that may require medical evaluation
- **Substance use:** Suspected or known drug or alcohol use by a minor
- **Trauma exposure:** Your child has experienced or witnessed violence, abuse, a disaster, or other traumatic event

This list is not exhaustive. **When in doubt, consult a professional.** Your pediatrician, family doctor, or a licensed child psychologist is always the right first call when something feels serious.
